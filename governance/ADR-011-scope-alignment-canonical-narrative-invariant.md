# ADR | Scope Alignment — Canonical Narrative Invariant

---

## Context

By Session 10, content population was underway across Projects, Experience,
and Method sections. A pattern emerged: individual pages risked drifting
toward discipline-specific framing rather than reinforcing the structural
method that unifies the site.

CJK also reflected on a job market observation: someone at his actual
experience level is better served by a coherent generalist systems narrative
than by scaffolding toward positions requiring qualifications not yet held.
The site needed a governing document that would hold all content to a single
testable standard.

---

## Decision

The Canonical Narrative Invariant is adopted as the site-wide content
governance standard. It is locked as KH-018.

**Core thesis:**

The KnowledgeHub documents a long-running systems practice centred on a
single question: what allows a complex thing to hold together as conditions
change?

This question governs all content across domains, mediums, and project types.
The site presents evidence of a stable structural method applied across
broadcasting, education, print, systems design, AI infrastructure, governed
workflows, and documentation systems. The visible diversity of work must
always resolve into this underlying continuity.

**Primary scope goal:**

Transform breadth into a coherent systems narrative. Every page must reinforce
that the same operating logic persists across changing environments,
stakeholders, constraints, technologies, and time horizons.

The governing scope is: the design, governance, and evolution of structures
that remain coherent under real-world change.

---

## Section-Level Alignment Contracts

### Home
Function: Introduce the overarching systems thesis.
Scope: Why this structural method matters in current AI, workflow, and
infrastructure contexts.
Must signal: long-term continuity, current AI systems relevance, governed
complexity, trust through structure.

### Projects
Function: Show the method operating in reality.
Scope: How complex systems are designed to remain coherent as requirements
evolve.
Must signal: decisions, dependencies, execution paths, operational
sustainability, architectural integrity.
Each project must expose: what needed to hold together, what changed, and
how coherence was preserved.

### Experience
Function: Show where the method was forged.
Scope: Longitudinal evidence of the same structural logic across domains.
Must signal: transferability, maturity, resilience under varied constraints,
system continuity over time.
Chronology is secondary. Structural continuity is primary.

### Method
Function: Reveal the causal engine behind the visible work.
Scope: The operating model that turns complexity into coherent systems.
Must signal: principles, explicit decisions, structural rigour, tool-independent
logic, reproducibility.
This is the unification layer of the site.

### Credentials
Function: Show what sharpened the method.
Scope: Formal learning layers that strengthened the structural operating model.
Must signal: reproducibility, governance, systems literacy, evaluation
discipline, computational rigour.
Passive certificate listing is not permitted. Learning must always be
interpreted through the method.

### About
Function: Humanise the persistence of the question.
Scope: Why this systems question has remained central across a sustained
interdisciplinary practice.
Must signal: intellectual continuity, curiosity about complexity, design trust
through structure, reason for current AI focus.
This is the human continuity layer, not biography.

### Legacy
Function: Show method evolution over time.
Scope: Earlier expressions of the same structural logic.
Must signal: provenance, continuity, development, conceptual lineage.
Legacy should feel like earlier system states, not old work.

---

## Global Writing Rule

Every page must survive this challenge:

Can this page be read as evidence of how a complex thing is made to hold
together as conditions change?

If the answer is no, the framing has drifted from scope. This is the site-wide
invariant.

---

## Editorial QA Checklist

Before publishing any page, confirm:

- Does the page reinforce the core thesis?
- Does it prioritise structure over discipline labels?
- Does it expose decisions, dependencies, or constraints?
- Does it show continuity under changing conditions?
- Does it fit its section-level contract?
- Does it strengthen the Home to Projects to Method arc?

---

## Alternatives Considered

**Discipline-led framing** -- rejected. Framing individual sections around
domains (broadcasting, AI, education) fragments the site into a portfolio of
unrelated work. It does not communicate the structural continuity that is
the actual differentiator.

**Seniority-led framing** -- rejected. Positioning toward roles requiring
qualifications not yet held creates misalignment with the actual job market
position and risks disqualifying applications rather than attracting them.

**No governing invariant** -- rejected. Without a testable standard, content
drift is unavoidable over a multi-session build. The invariant provides a
per-page quality gate that does not depend on editorial memory.

---

## Rationale

The core insight is that breadth is the asset, but only if it resolves into
coherence. A site that presents 26 years of varied work without a unifying
frame reads as inconsistency. The same work, presented through the lens of
a consistent structural method applied across changing contexts, reads as
evidence of durable capability.

The Global Writing Rule is the operative test. It is domain-agnostic, format-
agnostic, and does not require editorial judgment about what counts as
"technical enough" or "senior enough". If a page cannot be read as evidence
of how a complex thing holds together under changing conditions, it has not
been framed correctly.

---

## Non-Goals / Constraints

- The invariant governs framing and scope, not content accuracy or factual
  claims.
- It does not prohibit technical depth. It requires that technical depth be
  connected to the structural argument.
- Section contracts are binding at the framing level. They do not dictate
  sentence-level copy choices.

---

## Consequences

- All existing and future pages must be validated against the Global Writing
  Rule before commit.
- Section-level contracts function as per-section briefs for content
  production.
- The editorial QA checklist is added to the pre-publish workflow.
- Any IA change, new page, or section rewrite requires validation against
  this invariant before implementation.
