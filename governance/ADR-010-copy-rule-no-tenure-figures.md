# ADR | Copy Rule — No Tenure Figures in Body Copy

---

## Context

Across the first content sessions, the phrase "26 years" or "26-year" appeared
repeatedly in body copy as a credibility signal. The phrase was present in the
Method index, Experience framing, About section contract, and several other
places. By Session 10, it had appeared enough times to function as a formula
rather than a meaningful claim.

The question arose whether the tenure figure was earning its place or becoming
a reflex.

---

## Decision

No tenure figures — "26 years", "26-year", or equivalent numerical duration
claims — appear in body copy anywhere on the site, in either language.

The domain list (broadcasting, education, print, systems design, AI
infrastructure) carries the weight of breadth. The structural argument carries
the weight of depth. The number adds nothing that the evidence does not already
demonstrate.

This rule applies to all sections, all pages, both English and German.

**Governance ID:** COP-001

---

## Alternatives Considered

**Retain the figure selectively** -- rejected. Once a rule has exceptions, it
requires editorial judgment on every page. The rule is cleaner and more
enforceable as an absolute.

**Use the figure in the About section only** -- rejected. The About section is
the human continuity layer, not biography. A tenure count in that context
shifts the register toward CV summary, which is not the intended function.

---

## Rationale

A figure that appears on every page stops functioning as information and starts
functioning as filler. The domain list across broadcasting, education, print,
systems design, and AI infrastructure communicates breadth without a number.
A reader who counts or estimates the span from the evidence is more engaged
than one who is handed the arithmetic.

Removing the figure also removes an implicit seniority claim that is not the
positioning. The KnowledgeHub is not selling tenure. It is selling structural
method.

---

## Non-Goals / Constraints

- This rule does not apply to the GitHub profile, LinkedIn profile, or CV, where
  a tenure figure may be appropriate to the format and audience convention.
- It does not prohibit temporal language entirely. Phrases like "over time",
  "sustained practice", or "across decades" are acceptable where contextually
  earned.

---

## Consequences

- All existing body copy must be reviewed for tenure figures before commit.
- The About section scope sentence was updated in the same session: "across a
  sustained interdisciplinary practice" replaces "across a 26-year practice".
- Future content passes must not reintroduce tenure figures without explicit
  approval and a superseding decision.

---

## Supersedes

None.

---

## Review Trigger

Revisit only if a specific audience or format context makes a tenure figure
materially useful and no alternative framing achieves the same effect.
